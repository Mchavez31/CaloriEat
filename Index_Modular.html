    Welcome to CaloriEat! This comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your nutrition tracking experience.

    üöÄ Getting Started

    First Time Setup

    1. Create Account: Sign up with a username and password
    2. Complete Profile: Enter your display name, email, birthdate, height, weight, and gender
    3. Set Your Goals: Navigate to "Set Goals" to define your daily targets
    4. Start Tracking: Begin logging meals and tracking your progress!

    üìä Dashboard

    Progress Rings

    Six color-coded rings display your daily progress:

    • Calories: Total calorie intake vs. your goal
    • Protein: Protein intake in grams
    • Carbs: Carbohydrate intake in grams
    • Fat: Fat intake in grams
    • Sugar: Sugar intake in grams (tracked separately for health monitoring)
    • Veggies: Vegetable servings (1 serving = 1 cup / 90g)

    üí° Tip: Rings display a green "surpass" halo when you exceed your daily goal!

    Meal History

    • Today's Meals: View all meals logged today by default
    • View All History: Toggle to see your complete meal history across all dates
    • Edit/Delete: Click any meal to edit or remove it
    • Clear Selected: Deselect meals with the "Clear Selection" button

    Interactive Charts

    • Calorie Intake Chart: Track total calories over time
    • Meal Type Chart: See breakdown by Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Snacks
    • Weight Tracking: Monitor your weight progress over time
    • Time Period Filters: Toggle between Overall, Yearly, Monthly, Weekly, and Daily views
    • Fullscreen Mode: Click the fullscreen button (‚õ∂) to expand any chart for detailed viewing
    • Interactive Data: Hover over chart points to see exact values

    üéØ Set Goals

    Setting Your Calorie Goal

    Choose your diet goal (Maintain Weight, Lose Weight, or Gain Weight) and enter your target daily calories. Here are some general guidelines:

    • Maintain Weight: Eat around your maintenance calories (typically your BMR √ó activity factor)
    • Lose Weight: Create a calorie deficit of 300-500 calories below maintenance (targeting 0.5-1 lb/week loss)
    • Gain Weight: Create a calorie surplus of 300-500 calories above maintenance (targeting 0.5-1 lb/week gain)

    üí° Tip: Use an online BMR calculator or consult with a healthcare provider to determine your ideal calorie target based on your age, gender, height, weight, and activity level.

    Setting Macro Goals

    After setting your calorie goal, enter your macro targets. Here are evidence-based recommendations:

    • Protein: Recommended 0.8-1.2g per kg of body weight (higher for active individuals)
    • Carbs: Typically 45-65% of total calories (4 calories per gram)
    • Fat: Typically 20-35% of total calories (9 calories per gram)
    • Sugar: AHA recommends limiting added sugar to max 25g (women) or 36g (men) per day
    • Veggies: CDC recommends 2-3 cups (servings) per day for optimal health

    üí° Tip: Your goals are automatically saved and persist across sessions! You can adjust them anytime as your needs change.

    üçΩÔ∏è Add Meals

    Food Database Search (Recommended)

    Search our comprehensive database of 15,000+ foods:

    1. Type food name in the search box (e.g., "broccoli", "chicken breast", "McDonald's burger")
    2. Browse results with nutrition info and category badges
    3. Click any food to auto-fill all fields (calories, macros, and more)
    4. Vegetables are automatically detected - the veggies field fills to 1 serving automatically!
    5. Select meal type (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, or Snack)
    6. Choose meal date (defaults to today, or select past date for forgotten meals)
    7. Click "Save Meal" to log it

    üí° Tip: Database includes popular restaurant items from McDonald's, Subway, Chipotle, and more!

    Manual Entry

    If food isn't in database, enter manually:

    1. Enter meal name
    2. Fill in calories, protein, carbs, fat, and sugar
    3. For vegetables: Enter servings (1 serving = 1 cup = 90g)
    4. Select meal type and date
    5. Click "Save Meal"

    Meal Types

    • Breakfast: Morning meals
    • Lunch: Midday meals
    • Dinner: Evening meals
    • Snack: Any snacks or small meals between main meals

    Track meal timing to see your calorie distribution throughout the day in the Meal Type Chart!

    Vegetable Tracking

    • Auto-Detection: When you select a vegetable from the database, the veggies field automatically fills to 1 serving
    • Serving Size: 1 serving = 1 cup = 90 grams
    • Conversion Display: As you type servings, see the gram equivalent (e.g., "1.5 cups = 135g")
    • Manual Override: Adjust the auto-filled value if needed (0.5 for half cup, 2 for two cups, etc.)

    Custom Date Selection

    • Default: Meals save to today's date automatically
    • Past Dates: Select a previous date to log forgotten meals
    • Auto-Reset: Date picker resets to today after each meal is saved

    üí° Tip: Weigh yourself at the same time each day (ideally morning, after bathroom, before eating) for most accurate tracking!

    üë§ Account

    Profile Information

    • Email: Contact email for your records
    • Birthdate: Your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format)

    ‚ú® Tips & Best Practices

    • Consistency is Key: Log meals consistently for accurate tracking and better insights
    • Use Food Search: Save time by using the database instead of manual entry
    • Track Everything: Don't forget snacks, drinks with calories, and cooking oils

    üîß Troubleshooting

    Common Issues

    • Data Not Saving: Check browser settings - localStorage must be enabled
    • Charts Not Loading: Refresh the page or check internet connection for Chart.js library
    • Food Search Not Working: Database loads on page load - wait a few seconds and try again
    • Progress Rings Wrong: Verify meals are logged with correct values and to correct date. Double-check that your goals are set correctly on the Set Goals page.
    • Meals Not Appearing: Make sure you clicked "Add Meal" after entering all required fields. Check that you're viewing the correct date (Today's Meals vs. View All History).
